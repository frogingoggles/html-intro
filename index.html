Hospital Medicine Counterintuition

It is harmful to intensify antihypertensive regimen in hospitalized patients or give PRN bp meds

  • Intensification of therapy without signs of end-organ damage was associated with worse outcomes
  • Treatment of episodic hypertension in the hospital is associated with severe adverse effects

Evidence does not suggest abnormal urine analyses are significant in absence of urinary symptoms

  • Treating Asymptomatic UA Increases Hospital Length Of Stay With No Benefit
  • Not treating bacteruria in patients with altered mental status rarely leads to worsening infection

Contrast Nephropathy from ct scan contrast is rare

  • The American College of Radiology and the National Kidney Foundation say contrast is low risk if gfr > 30 (and likely overstated altogether)
  • No contrast nephropathy occurred from cta pe in cohort

There is significant harm in using antipsychotics in delirious elderly patients

  • Antipsychotic use compared with non-use in adults with dementia was associated with increased risks of stroke, venous thromboembolism, myocardial infarction, heart failure, fracture, pneumonia, and acute kidney injury

Other counterintuitive facts

  • Intervene on urinary retention when retaining more than 500 ml of urine
  • Outcome may be better for faster correction of hyponatremia
  • Sepsis alerts are not accurate
  • Living in rural areas increases mortality
  • Boarding in the ER is deadly if you can't do ADLs
